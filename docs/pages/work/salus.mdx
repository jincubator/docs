# Salus

Salus is an active trading-system research and reference implementation led and implemented by John Whitton. It is not commercially validated or production-proven profitable.

## Problem

Trading systems must turn changing market data into candidate routes, evaluate those routes quickly, and preserve a clear boundary between a modelled signal and an executable outcome.

## Approach

Salus separates market-data ingestion, graph construction, candidate-route generation, route evaluation, simulation, and controlled execution boundaries. This makes each stage testable without equating throughput or a simulated result with realized value.

## What John built

John leads and implements the system, its walkthroughs, and the retained measurement workflow.

The implementation spans a 13-crate Rust workspace and 16 walkthrough documents. A retained Ethereum snapshot contains 3,415 token nodes, 4,552 pool components, and 2,401,108 generated max-four-hop routes.

## Technical challenges

Generated routes are candidate paths. They are not evaluated routes, filtered opportunities, submitted or included executions, or realized after-gas profit. Salus keeps those states distinct while processing changing graph data and bounded route sets.

## Retained results

The measurements below come from retained simulation and replay artifacts dated July 9, 2026. They were re-read by the audited code rather than produced by new live market runs.

| --- | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: |
| Ethereum · 100 blocks | 486,934 | 4,554 | 7,528 | 0 |
| Base · 500 blocks | 1,279,758 | 16,537 | 38,462 | 0 |
| Unichain · 1,004 blocks | 100,714 | 3,259 | 21,867 | 0 |

Sustained throughput is aggregate evaluated-route count divided by measured service time across a retained run. Peak is the highest observed per-window rate.

## Limitations

The three cited runs selected zero routes after gas and all configured gates. Throughput does not establish opportunity recall, execution success, realized profit, current live performance, or commercial validation.

Flash liquidity can remove the need to pre-fund trading principal. Infrastructure, data/RPC, operational, and transaction-gas costs still exist; reverted, unsuccessful, or non-included attempts may leave unreimbursed costs.

The public material does not disclose current provider policy, fee policy, thresholds, prioritization, route selection, opportunity values, or submission strategy.
