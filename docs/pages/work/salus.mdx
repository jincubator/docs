# Salus

Salus is an active Jincubator research and engineering initiative and reference implementation for decentralized trading-system architecture.

## Problem

Trading systems must turn changing market data into candidate routes, evaluate those routes within changing time constraints, and preserve the difference between a modeled signal and an executable outcome. A fast catalog or evaluator is not enough: evidence must remain attributable to a particular revision and retained workload, and each stage must be reviewable without implying success at a later stage.

Salus is an active engineering initiative and reference implementation. Commercial validation remains ongoing. Its present evidence does not establish a production trading platform or profitable after-gas production execution.

## Approach

Salus separates market-state ingestion, inspectable persistence and read models, graph construction, bounded route catalogs, route evaluation, simulation, and controlled execution gates. This makes each stage testable without equating throughput or a simulated result with realized value.

The Salus implementation repository owns current behavior and implementation detail. This public case study uses a reviewed, commit-scoped snapshot instead of copying living walkthroughs or operational documentation.

The reviewed implementation comprises a 13-crate Rust workspace and a 16-document walkthrough corpus. Its engineering surface includes ingestion and decoded market state, inspectable read models, graph and route catalogs, economic evaluation, simulation and preflight, controlled execution boundaries, queue-backed orchestration, and operator evidence.

This artifact explains that system boundary and its retained observations. It does not reproduce implementation documentation, operational configuration, current strategy behavior, or private evidence.

## Technical challenges

A catalog route, an evaluated route, an observed opportunity, a selected after-gas route, a submitted transaction, an execution, and realized profit are different facts. Salus keeps those states distinct while processing changing graph data and bounded route sets.

Separate crates, read models, evaluation stages, and execution gates improve diagnosis and review. The trade-off is more explicit coordination between components and more evidence before a result can advance from evaluation toward execution. Throughput and catalog size describe implemented capacity and retained workloads; they do not measure opportunity recall, execution success, or profitability.

Salus does not require upfront trading principal for the modeled transaction structure. It still requires infrastructure, RPC, operational, and gas expenditure, and unsuccessful transactions may incur unreimbursed costs.

## Limitations

- Profitable after-gas production execution has not been established.
- Commercial validation remains ongoing.
- Retained results are dated observations at a pinned revision, not guarantees of present or future performance.
- Catalog routes, route evaluations, opportunities, selected after-gas routes, submitted transactions, executions, and realized profit are not interchangeable metrics.
- Implementation and operational maturity continue to evolve in the Salus repository.
- Public material excludes provider configuration, gas-bidding logic, thresholds, route-selection details, opportunity identities or values, active strategy parameters, vulnerabilities, and other sensitive implementation behavior.
